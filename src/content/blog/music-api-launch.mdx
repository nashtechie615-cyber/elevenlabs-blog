ElevenLabs just opened up its Music API to developers, and it does more than generate background loops. You can now hit a single endpoint, pass a text prompt describing genre, mood, instrumentation, and duration, and get back a full audio track — streamed or returned as a complete file. That's a meaningful shift for anyone building apps that need original music without a licensing headache.

## [What the API Actually Does](https://try.elevenlabs.io/8jq9xhb1mno4)

The endpoint accepts a prompt-based request. You describe what you want — something like "upbeat lo-fi hip hop with warm piano, 90 BPM, relaxed study mood" — and the model handles the rest. You can control approximate track length, which matters when you're generating music that needs to fit a specific scene or content slot.

Streaming is supported, so you don't have to wait for the full track to finish generating before playback starts. For real-time applications or anything where latency is visible to the user, that's a practical advantage over batch-only approaches.

The output is a clean audio file. No watermarks in the commercial tier. The tracks generated through [ElevenLabs Music API](https://try.elevenlabs.io/8jq9xhb1mno4) come with commercial licensing attached, which means you can use them in products, videos, podcasts, or client work without negotiating rights separately.

## [Prompt Parameters Worth Knowing](https://try.elevenlabs.io/8jq9xhb1mno4)

Genre and mood descriptors do the heavy lifting here. The model responds well to specific language — naming an instrument, a tempo feel, or an emotional tone gets you closer to a usable result than vague prompts. Think "melancholic acoustic guitar, fingerpicked, slow tempo" rather than just "sad music."

Length control is coarse rather than sample-precise, but for most use cases — short video clips, app onboarding screens, podcast intros — the available range covers what you need. If you need exact timing down to the millisecond, you'll still want a DAW in the loop. But for programmatic generation at scale, this is workable.

## [What You Can Actually Build](https://try.elevenlabs.io/8jq9xhb1mno4)

A few practical directions:

- **Video tools** — Auto-generate background music matched to a video's mood or category without touching a music library.
- **Podcast platforms** — Let creators generate a custom intro track in seconds instead of hunting royalty-free sites.
- **Games and apps** — Dynamically generate ambient music that adapts to in-app states or user preferences.
- **Content automation pipelines** — Pair the Music API with the voice API to produce fully narrated, scored audio content end to end.

The commercial license is the part that makes most of these actually viable for production use. Without it, you're back to clearing rights or using library music.

If you're building anything in the audio or video space and want to cut the music licensing problem out entirely, [start here](https://try.elevenlabs.io/8jq9xhb1mno4) and run a few test prompts. The API is live and the barrier to a first track is low.
