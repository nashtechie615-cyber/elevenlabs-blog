## [How to Prompt for Podcast Music](https://try.elevenlabs.io/8jq9xhb1mno4)

The quality of your output depends almost entirely on how specific your prompt is. Vague prompts produce generic results. Specific prompts produce usable tracks.

Start with genre. Then add instrumentation. Then describe the emotional texture. Then note tempo or energy level if it matters.

A weak prompt: *background music for a podcast*

A stronger prompt: *lo-fi hip hop, warm Rhodes piano, soft brushed drums, relaxed and focused, moderate tempo, suitable for a business interview show*

Even stronger: add context like "fades out naturally" or "builds slightly in the middle" if you want the structure to do something specific.

Genre tags that tend to work well for podcasts include lo-fi, ambient, cinematic, acoustic, jazz-influenced, and minimal electronic. For stingers, think short bursts — try prompts like "five-second drum fill and synth sting, punchy, energetic" and iterate from there.

Tracks can run up to a few minutes depending on your settings, which is more than enough for most podcast music needs. For intros and outros, aim for fifteen to thirty seconds. Transitions rarely need more than five.
