For years, the answer to "can we run ElevenLabs without sending audio to the cloud?" was polite and firm: no. That's changed. [ElevenLabs now offers three deployment modes](https://try.elevenlabs.io/8jq9xhb1mno4) that keep voice data entirely inside the customer's own infrastructure — and the use cases this unlocks are the ones that have been stuck on procurement approval for the last two years.

## [Three flavors of "not in our cloud"](https://try.elevenlabs.io/8jq9xhb1mno4)

**Virtual Private Cloud.** The fastest path to ship. Models run inside your AWS SageMaker or GCP Vertex environment, meaning audio, transcripts, and voice data never leave your account. ElevenLabs has no access to logs, no ability to pull data, no visibility into what you're processing. This option is generally available today.

**On-premise.** The full models running on your own GPU-equipped hardware in your own data center, backed by Confidential Computing infrastructure. This is the option you buy when your compliance team has already written the word "air-gapped" on the RFP.

**On-device.** The models compiled to run directly on the target hardware — no network call required. Think automotive head units, wearables, and industrial equipment that has to work offline in the field.

## [Who actually needs this](https://try.elevenlabs.io/8jq9xhb1mno4)

Government agencies top the list. When your jurisdiction mandates that citizen data stays within national borders and commercial cloud providers don't offer a region that qualifies, on-premise is the only real option. Financial services and healthcare follow close behind — any workload where the audit trail matters more than the deployment speed.

On-device opens a different category entirely: voice interfaces in vehicles, hearing aids with on-chip noise isolation, field equipment in mining and logistics where connectivity is a luxury. These were never served by a cloud-first product.

Custom voice development is part of the package, and so is language and dialect fine-tuning for regionally specific needs. The local editions are being rolled out through the first half of 2026, with VPC live now and on-premise plus on-device entering early access.
