## [The Workflow That Actually Scales](https://try.elevenlabs.io/8jq9xhb1mno4)

Start with your source script, not the video. Export a clean text version of every line of narration before you touch anything else. This matters because translation works on text, not on exported audio, and you want a single source of truth you can version-control.

From there the steps are straightforward. Feed the script into a translation tool — there are several capable options — and generate target-language versions for whichever markets you're prioritizing. Keep formatting consistent: same paragraph breaks, same line numbers. That alignment will save you time during sync.

Next, bring those translated scripts into [ElevenLabs](https://try.elevenlabs.io/8jq9xhb1mno4). The key move here is using the same voice identity across every language. ElevenLabs supports multilingual generation with cloned or synthetic voices, which means your Spanish narration and your Japanese narration can carry the same recognizable vocal character as the English original. That consistency isn't cosmetic — it keeps the brand feel intact across markets without requiring a separate voice actor for each locale.

Generate each language version as an audio file, then drop them into your video editor against the existing timeline.

## [Where Human Review Still Matters](https://try.elevenlabs.io/8jq9xhb1mno4)

Automated translation is fast. It is not perfect. Before you publish anything, a native speaker needs to read the script — not just the audio, the actual text — and flag anything that sounds technically correct but culturally off. Product terminology is a common problem. Terms like "dashboard," "workflow," or "pipeline" translate differently depending on industry context in the target language, and a machine won't know which register your audience expects.

Timing is the other thing to check manually. Different languages expand or compress at different rates relative to English. A sentence that takes four seconds in English might run six seconds in German or two and a half in Mandarin. You may need to trim pauses, adjust clip length, or re-time on-screen text to match.

Build that review step into your weekend timeline, not as an afterthought. Budget a few hours per language for a native reviewer to sign off on both the script and the synced cut.

That's a meaningful difference when someone in Seoul or São Paulo is deciding whether your product is worth a closer look.
