Most people have a graveyard of PDFs they meant to read. Research papers, long-form articles, saved newsletters, ebooks that never got opened. ElevenReader is a straightforward answer to that problem: turn any of those files into audio and listen while you do something else.

## [What ElevenReader Actually Does](https://try.elevenlabs.io/8jq9xhb1mno4)

The app is free and built around one core idea — import a document, pick a voice, and listen. You can pull in PDFs, EPUBs, web articles, and plain text files. Once the content is in, ElevenReader converts it using the same voice synthesis technology that powers ElevenLabs' professional tools.

That matters because the audio quality isn't the flat, robotic output you get from a phone's built-in read-aloud feature. The voices handle punctuation, sentence rhythm, and natural pacing in a way that makes longer content genuinely comfortable to listen to over time.

## [Voice Selection and Playback Controls](https://try.elevenlabs.io/8jq9xhb1mno4)

You're not locked into a single default voice. ElevenReader gives you access to a library of voices so you can pick something that fits the material — or just something you find easier to listen to for an hour. Different voices work better for different content types. A dense academic paper might benefit from a slower, measured delivery. A news article might work better at a faster clip.

Speed controls let you push playback faster or slower depending on how familiar you are with the subject. Bookmarking means you can drop a pin mid-chapter and come back to exactly where you left off. These aren't revolutionary features on their own, but the combination works well for the actual use case: interrupted commutes, gym sessions, lunch breaks.

## [Who This Is For](https://try.elevenlabs.io/8jq9xhb1mno4)

If you already use something like Pocket or Instapaper to save articles you never read, [ElevenReader](https://try.elevenlabs.io/8jq9xhb1mno4) fits into that same workflow — except the backlog actually gets cleared. Students dealing with dense reading lists, professionals trying to stay current in their field, or anyone who simply absorbs information better through audio will find it useful.
