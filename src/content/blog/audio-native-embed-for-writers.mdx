## [What Audio Native Actually Does](https://try.elevenlabs.io/8jq9xhb1mno4)

Audio Native is a lightweight embed that converts your written content into a hosted audio player. You drop a small snippet of code into your site's template — once — and ElevenLabs handles the rest. It crawls each page, generates a narrated version using a voice you choose, and serves it through a player that lives right inside your post.

The player is unobtrusive. It sits above or below your content, shows a waveform, and lets readers scrub through or adjust playback speed. No separate app, no podcast feed required on your end.

## [The Part That Actually Saves Time](https://try.elevenlabs.io/8jq9xhb1mno4)

The feature most writers underestimate is automatic sync. When you update an article — fix a typo, rewrite a section, add a paragraph — Audio Native detects the change and regenerates the audio. You don't manually re-export or re-upload anything. The player on your live post will reflect the updated version the next time it's requested.

For anyone running a news site or a publication that regularly corrects or updates posts, this matters. Stale audio that contradicts edited text is worse than no audio at all.

## [Voice Attribution and Paywall Support](https://try.elevenlabs.io/8jq9xhb1mno4)

If you have multiple contributors, you can assign different voices to different authors. A reader who follows a specific columnist can start recognizing their voice the same way they recognize their byline. It's a small thing, but it adds consistency across a large publication.

On the paywall side, Audio Native integrates with access controls so that gated content stays gated. The audio player won't expose text to someone who hasn't authenticated. If your site already restricts content by login or subscription tier, the audio version follows the same rules. You're not accidentally opening a back channel around your paywall.

Setup requires pasting one embed snippet into your site's head or article template, then configuring your preferences — default voice, player position, which pages to include or exclude — through the dashboard.

That's genuinely it. No custom API calls, no audio editing software, no manual uploads per post.
