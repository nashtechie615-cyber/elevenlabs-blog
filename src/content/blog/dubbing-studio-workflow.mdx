Most creators who want to reach international audiences hit the same wall: hire voice actors for every language, spend weeks coordinating, and end up with a dubbed version that sounds nothing like them. ElevenLabs Dubbing Studio takes a different approach. It clones your voice, translates your script, and syncs the output — all inside one workflow.

Here's exactly how to do it.


<Video src="/resources/elevenlabs-lines-animated.mp4" caption="See ElevenLabs in action." ratio="16/9" />

## [Upload Your Video and Let It Transcribe](https://try.elevenlabs.io/8jq9xhb1mno4)

Start by uploading your video directly to [Dubbing Studio](https://try.elevenlabs.io/8jq9xhb1mno4). The platform accepts common video formats and pulls the audio track automatically. From there, it transcribes your speech and segments it into speaker turns.

If your video has multiple speakers, the system attempts to separate them. Each speaker gets their own lane in the editor. You can rename them, adjust the transcript, and fix any transcription errors before moving forward. This step matters — garbage in, garbage out. A clean transcript means cleaner translations downstream.

## [Voice Matching Across Every Language](https://try.elevenlabs.io/8jq9xhb1mno4)

This is where Dubbing Studio earns its keep. Rather than swapping your voice for a generic text-to-speech engine, it uses your existing audio as a voice reference. The output in Spanish, French, German, Japanese, or any of the other supported languages sounds like *you* — same tone, same cadence, same energy.

The translation happens automatically, but you're not locked out of it. Every translated line is editable inside the studio interface. If a phrase sounds awkward in the target language, you can rewrite it before the audio is generated. This is especially useful for idiomatic content — jokes, slang, or culture-specific references that don't translate literally.

Once you're satisfied, you generate the dubbed audio. The system attempts to match timing to your original video so lip movements don't fall wildly out of sync.

## [Review, Export, and What It Costs](https://try.elevenlabs.io/8jq9xhb1mno4)

The review stage lets you play back each segment and regenerate individual lines if needed. You're not stuck re-running the entire video if one sentence comes out wrong.

Export options include the dubbed video with the new audio baked in, or the audio track alone if you want to handle the video editing yourself.

On cost: ElevenLabs charges based on minutes of dubbed content produced, and pricing scales with your subscription tier. A few minutes of content per language is realistic to produce in under an hour once you're familiar with the interface. Running twelve languages on a ten-minute video will consume a meaningful chunk of credits, so plan accordingly and prioritize your highest-traffic markets first.

The workflow isn't magic — you still need to review translations and spot-check the output. But compared to coordinating twelve separate voiceover projects, it's a significant reduction in both time and cost.
